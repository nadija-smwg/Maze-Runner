## What is MicroMaze?
MicroMaze is an inter-university competition aimed at challenging participants to design and develop a micro mouse capable of efficiently navigating and solving a maze within a designated time frame. The primary objective is to engineer a micro mouse that successfully completes the maze within the allotted period. This event evaluates students' proficiency in robotics, programming, and analytical problem-solving.

Following the remarkable success of previous editions, MicroMaze III aims to deliver an even larger and more impactful experience. It features enhanced challenges, expanded opportunities for sponsorships, partnerships, networking, and industry exposure.

## Micromouse - Rules and Requirements
* **2.2.1 Self-Containment:** The Micromouse shall be completely self-contained, fully autonomous, and receive no outside assistance.
* **2.2.2 Power Source:** No combustion processes or emitted pollutants. Voltage between any 2 points should not exceed 24V at any given time.
* **2.2.3 Size:** Maximum length of 14.5cm, maximum width of 14.5cm. No height restrictions.
* **2.2.4 Weight:** Must remain constant. Battery replacements must keep weight within +/- 5g of the initial weight.
* **2.2.5 Locomotion:** Cannot jump over, fly over, climb, scratch, cut, burn, mark, damage, or destroy walls.
* **2.2.6 Dislodged Parts:** Should not leave any part of its body behind.
* **2.2.7 External Communication:** Wireless communication is strictly prohibited. Modules with wireless capabilities must have them explicitly disabled (hardware or software).
* **2.2.8 Operation Modes:** Manual switches allowed to change modes/algorithms. Reprogramming code is not allowed after initial inspection.

---

## Rules for the Maze
* **2.3.1 Dimensions:** 18cm x 18cm unit cells arranged as a 16 x 16 grid. Walls are 5 cm high and 1.2 cm thick (5% tolerance).
* **2.3.2 Construction:** PVC sheets with non-gloss black sticker floor. Walls are white with a red top edge. Floor bumps up to ±2 mm may exist.
* **2.3.3 & 2.3.4 Conditions:** Ambient room conditions. Do not make assumptions about lighting.
* **2.3.5 Lattice Points:** 1.2 cm x 1.2 cm posts at the four corners of each unit cell.
* **2.3.6 Start/End:** Starting cell at a corner (bounded by walls on three sides). Destination goal is a four-cell area near the center with one entry point.

---

## Rules for the Qualifier Round
* **2.4.1 Initial Inspection:** Teams must pass inspection by judges.
* **2.4.2 Trial Time:** 8 minutes of access to the maze.
* **2.4.3 Runs:** Maximum of 5 attempts ("Runs") during the 8 minutes.
* **2.4.5 Run Time:** Measured from Start Line to Finish Line. Slowest teams are eliminated.
* **2.4.6 Penalties:**
    * *Contact Penalty:* 3-second penalty per collision with walls (successive collisions within 3s count as one).
    * *Reset Penalty:* 20-second penalty for manually lifting the mouse from the destination to start.
* **2.4.7 Restarting:** If physically stuck, can be manually placed at the start, terminating the run without a recorded time.
* **2.4.8 Adjusting:** Reprogramming prohibited once maze is revealed. Physical repairs/adjustments require permission.

---

## Rules for the Final Round
* **2.5.1 Initial Inspection:** Robots placed in an isolation zone before maze reveal.
* **2.5.2 Competition Time:** 8 minutes of access.
* **2.5.3 Runs:** Maximum of 10 attempts.
* **2.5.4 Run Time:** Shortest Official Time wins.
* **2.5.5 Penalties:** 3-second contact penalty. 20-second manual reset penalty.
* **2.5.7 Adjusting:** Similar to qualifiers; memory/microcontroller replacement requires judge permission and original code.

---

## Judging Discretion
The judges reserve the right to:
* Ask the operator for an explanation of the Micromouse and its actions.
* Stop a run, declare disqualification, or give instructions.
* Question you if the structure of the maze is jeopardized.
